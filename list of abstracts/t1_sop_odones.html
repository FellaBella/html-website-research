AgroSentinel: A Hybrid Bio-Robotic and Environmental Sensing System for Enhancing Typhoon Resilience in Rice Production

STATEMENT OF THE PROBLEM

  1. Rice production in the Philippines is frequently disrupted by typhoons and flooding, resulting in reduced yields and crop losses.
  2. Existing agricultural practices lack adaptive systems that can proactively respond to extreme weather conditions.
  3. Although nature-based solutions show potential in improving agricultural resilience, their application is limited due to the absence of technology-supported and dynamic frameworks.
  4. Smart sensing and automated agricultural systems remain mostly at the conceptual or pilot stage and are not widely implemented in real rice farming environments.
  5. There is a lack of scalable hybrid bio-robotic systems that integrate biological responsiveness with robotic automation for environmental sensing in rice production.
